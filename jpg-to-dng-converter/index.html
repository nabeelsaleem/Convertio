What Happens When You Convert Image to DNG?

It is crucial to manage expectations when dealing with file formats. When you utilize a jpg to dng converter, it is important to understand the technical limitations of the source file.

No Quality Gain: Converting a JPG to DNG does not turn it into a true RAW file. Because a JPG is already compressed and stripped of its original camera sensor data, the conversion process only "wraps" the existing compressed image into a DNG container. This means you will not gain editing flexibility, highlight recovery, or increased dynamic range. The conversion is primarily for workflow compatibility, not magical enhancement.

Why Turn JPG into DNG?

  • Unified Catalogs: Keeping all files in the same format prevents software errors during bulk exports or metadata synchronization.
  • Non-Destructive Editing: DNG containers hold metadata edits natively, whereas JPGs require annoying XML sidecar files to store Lightroom adjustments.
  • Software Requirements: Certain timelapse plugins strictly require DNG or RAW sequences and will reject JPGs outright.
