Understanding Your Net Income After Service Fees

In the modern gig economy, the price you charge a client is rarely the amount that ends up in your bank account. Whether you are a freelancer on Fiverr, a seller on Etsy, or a developer on the App Store, platform fees can take a significant bite out of your revenue. Our Earnings After Platform Fee Calculator is designed to provide instant clarity on your net income after service fees, helping you price your work more effectively and manage your business finances with precision.

Why Calculating Marketplace Fees Matters

Many new entrepreneurs make the mistake of calculating their profit based on the gross sale price. However, once you factor in a 10% or 20% service charge, plus fixed transaction costs, your actual margins might be much lower than expected. By using a dedicated platform fee calculator, you can reverse-engineer your pricing. If you know you need to take home $800 for a project and the platform takes 20%, you quickly realize you must charge at least $1,000.

This tool is particularly useful for comparing different marketplaces. For instance, while one platform might have a higher traffic volume, its higher service charge might make a lower-traffic, lower-fee alternative more profitable for your specific niche.

How to Use the Take-Home Pay Tool

Our calculator is built to be universal. To get an accurate result, follow these three simple steps:

  • Enter Gross Earnings: This is the total amount the customer pays or the project budget.
  • Input Platform Fee: Enter the percentage the marketplace deducts (e.g., 20 for Fiverr, 10 for Upwork).
  • Add Fixed Fees: Some platforms charge a flat fee per transaction (e.g., $0.30 or $0.45) in addition to the percentage.

The tool instantly updates to show your Net Income, the total dollar amount lost to fees, and your effective keep rate. This transparency is vital for anyone looking to maintain a sustainable freelance career or e-commerce business.

Common Platform Fee Structures

Different industries have different standards for service charges. Freelance marketplaces like Fiverr typically charge 20%, while Upwork has moved toward a flat 10% for most contracts. E-commerce platforms like Etsy charge a 6.5% transaction fee but add payment processing fees on top of that. Creative platforms like Patreon or OnlyFans also have their own tiered structures. Our tool allows you to save time by using presets or entering custom values for any niche platform you use.
