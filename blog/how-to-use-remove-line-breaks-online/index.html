Common Mistakes to Avoid

Look, even the best tools can't read your mind. If you paste text that is already formatted with specific indentations—like a poem or a piece of Python code—you need to be careful.

  • Ignoring the "Preserve Paragraphs" toggle: This is the #1 mistake. If you want to keep your structure but fix the broken lines *within* those paragraphs, you must check this.
  • Not checking for double spaces: Sometimes when you remove line breaks, you end up with two spaces where the break used to be. Most good tools (like ours) fix this automatically, but always give it a quick scan.
  • Forgeting to remove repetitive words: If you're copying from a PDF that had headers on every page, you might find the same title appearing in the middle of your text. Use a remove duplicate keywords tool logic to catch those.
