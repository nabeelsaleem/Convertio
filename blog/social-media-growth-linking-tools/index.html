TikTok & Instagram: Hashtags & Clean Captions

Let's talk about the "aesthetic" platforms. TikTok and Instagram are brutal because they are so visual. If your caption looks like a wall of text with messy hashtags scattered everywhere, people scroll right past.

I’m a big fan of the "hidden hashtag" look. But to get the algorithm to actually categorize your content, you need the right ones. I’ve been leaning heavily on a tiktok hashtag generator online to find trending but low-competition tags. If you use #fyp, you’re a small fish in a giant ocean. If you use a niche-specific tag found through a generator, you’re a big fish in a small pond.

And for the love of everything, please space out your captions. There is nothing worse than an Instagram caption that looks like a legal contract. I use an instagram caption spacer online to create that clean, breathable whitespace. It makes people actually read your CTA.

The Hashtag Strategy for 2026

  • The 3-3-3 Rule: 3 broad hashtags, 3 niche hashtags, and 3 brand-specific hashtags.
  • Avoid Banned Tags: Always run your list through a hashtag checker before posting.
  • Visual Hierarchy: Put your most important sentence first, then the spacer, then the "fluff."

The Link in Bio Strategy for 2026

We need to talk about the "Bio" for a second. In 2026, your bio isn't just a bio. It’s your digital storefront. If you have a YouTube channel, a newsletter, a shop, and a Twitter account, you can't just pick one.

The easiest way is to use an instagram caption spacer online. You just type your caption with the breaks you want, click convert, and paste it into Instagram. This avoids the "merging text" bug that happens when you type directly into the app.
