Basically, it’s a tool that takes two timestamps and tells you exactly how much time passed between them. But it’s more than just subtraction. A proper hours calculator handles the messy stuff—like converting 15 minutes into 0.25 hours so you can actually multiply it by your hourly rate.

Best Practices for Hours Tracking

Look, the tool is great, but your data is only as good as what you put in. I've seen people lose hundreds of dollars because they didn't keep a clean log.

First off, track in real-time. Don't try to remember what you did last Monday at 2:00 PM. You won't. You'll guess, and you'll probably guess low. Anyway, even if you just jot it down in a notes app and then clean it up later with a caption cleaner tool or something similar, just get the raw numbers down.

Then, double-check your AM/PM. I once billed a client for 22 hours because I accidentally put 8:00 AM to 6:00 AM instead of PM. They were... not thrilled. Use the hours calculator for beginners settings if you're worried about making that mistake; it usually flags weird entries.
