Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between static and dynamic QR codes? ‚ñ∂

Static QR codes have a permanent destination that cannot be changed. They're encoded directly and offer complete privacy. Dynamic QR codes use a short URL that redirects to your content, allowing you to change the destination anytime and track analytics.

How small can I print a QR code? ‚ñ∂

Minimum 2√ó2 cm (0.8√ó0.8 inches) for close scanning. For posters, divide scanning distance by 10 to get minimum size in centimeters. Always test before mass printing!

Can QR codes be customized with my brand colors? ‚ñ∂

Yes! Modern generators allow color customization while maintaining scannability. The key is ensuring sufficient contrast. Our tool includes automatic contrast checking.

Are QR codes secure? Can they be hacked? ‚ñ∂

The QR code itself is just data and cannot be "hacked." The security risk lies in where it points. Always verify your QR code links to your intended destination. For sensitive applications, use static codes you control completely.

What file format should I use for print vs. digital? ‚ñ∂

For print: Always use vector formats (SVG/EPS). For digital: PNG at 2x or 3x resolution works well. Never use low-res PNG for print‚Äîit becomes pixelated and unscannable.

Can I track how many people scan my QR code? ‚ñ∂

Only with dynamic QR codes. Static codes don't offer built-in analytics, but you can use a URL shortener like Bitly to track clicks on the destination URL.
