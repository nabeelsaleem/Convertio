What is percentage difference?

Basically, percentage difference is what we use when we want to compare two values and there’s no obvious "starting" or "ending" point. If you’re tracking your weight from January to February, that’s percentage change. But if you’re comparing your weight to your twin brother’s? That’s where you need this specific formula.

Key Takeaway

Use percentage difference when the order of the two numbers doesn't matter. If you flip the numbers and the result should stay the same, you're in the right place.

The math behind it is actually pretty simple once you see it laid out, but it's tedious to do on a napkin. You take the absolute difference between the two numbers, divide that by the average of those same two numbers, and then multiply by 100. Sounds easy, right? Step-by-Step Guide to Getting it Right

Okay, so let’s say you actually want to know how the math works. Maybe you're a student or you just like suffering. Either way, here is the breakdown of how to use the percentage difference formula.

Step 1: Find the Absolute Difference

Subtract one value from the other. It doesn't matter which one is first because we use the "absolute" value—which is just a fancy way of saying "make the number positive." If you get -5, it's just 5.

Step 2: Calculate the Average

Add the two numbers together and divide by 2. This is the part people usually forget. They try to divide by the first number, but that's for percentage change. Don't do that.

Step 3: The Big Division

Take your result from Step 1 and divide it by the result from Step 2. You'll get a decimal, probably something like 0.0842.

Step 4: Final Percentage

Multiply by 100. Boom. You've got your percentage difference. Or, you know, you could just use the percentage difference calculator and be done with it before you even finish reading this sentence.

Pro Tip: Watch Your Units!

Make sure both numbers are in the same unit. Don't compare grams to ounces or $47.50 to 5,000 cents. It sounds obvious, but I've seen it happen more than I'd like to admit. If you're working with text data, you might also want to remove line breaks from your raw data before processing.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between percentage difference and percentage change? ▶

It's all about direction. Percentage change is used when you have an "old" value and a "new" value (like a stock price going up). Percentage difference is used when you are comparing two things that exist at the same time and neither one is the "original" one.

Can percentage difference be negative? ▶

Technically, no. Since we use the absolute difference in the formula, the result is always expressed as a positive number. It just tells you how far apart the two values are, regardless of which one is bigger.

Why do we divide by the average? ▶

We use the average because it provides a neutral ground. If we divided by just one of the numbers, the result would change depending on which number we picked first. Using the average ensures the percentage difference stays the same no matter the order.

Is there a limit to the numbers I can use? ▶

Not really! Our percentage difference calculator can handle tiny decimals or massive billions. As long as you aren't trying to divide by zero (which is impossible anyway), you're good to go.
