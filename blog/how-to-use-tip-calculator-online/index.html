I've made plenty of mistakes when paying bills. Once, I accidentally tipped 20% on the *total* after tax, but the "etiquette" usually suggests tipping on the pre-tax amount. Does it matter that much? The "Rounding" Trap

Don't round down so much that the tip becomes insulting. If the tip calculator says $14.85 and you round down to $14.00 just to keep it even, you're essentially taking money away from the staff. I always round *up* to the nearest dollar. It's good karma. Plus, it makes the math on your bank statement look way cleaner.
