Step-by-Step Guide: How to use overtime calculator

So, you’ve landed on the tool page. What now? It’s actually pretty intuitive, but here is a quick overtime calculator guide to make sure you’re inputting the right data.

  1. Enter Your Base Hourly Rate: This is what you get paid for your standard 40 hours. If you're on salary, you'll need to divide your weekly pay by 40 first. For example, if you make $800 a week, your rate is $20.00.
  2. Input Regular Hours: Usually, this is 40. But some contracts start overtime after 35 or 38 hours. Make sure you check your specific agreement!

Frequently Asked Questions

How exactly is overtime calculated? ▶

In most cases, you take your hourly rate and multiply it by 1.5 for any hours worked over 40 in a single week. Our overtime calculator automates this by taking your base pay and hours as inputs and providing the total gross pay instantly.

What is the difference between 1.5x and 2.0x? ▶

1.5x (time-and-a-half) is the federal legal minimum for overtime. 2.0x (double time) is often paid for holidays, Sundays, or when an employee works an excessive number of consecutive hours, depending on company policy or state law.

Can I use this tool for a bi-weekly pay period? ▶

Yes, but you must calculate each week individually. Federal law requires overtime to be calculated based on a 7-day work week. You cannot average two weeks together to avoid paying overtime. Use the overtime calculator for each week separately and then add the totals.
