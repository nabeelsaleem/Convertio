Troubleshooting Common Issues

Sometimes the math feels wrong. Usually, it's not the tool—it's the input. If you're getting a weird negative number, you probably swapped the "Start" and "End" times. It happens. Or, if you're using a unix timestamp converter, make sure you're not mixing up seconds and milliseconds. That's a classic $1,000 mistake right there.

Also, remember that time zones are a nightmare. If you started a task in New York and finished it in London, a basic time duration calculator won't know that unless you adjust for the offset first. I usually just convert everything to UTC before I start plugging numbers in. It saves so much heart-ache.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I calculate time across multiple days? ▶

Absolutely. The time duration calculator allows you to select specific start and end dates. This is perfect for tracking long-haul projects or vacation time that spans several weeks.

What is the difference between a time duration and a timestamp? ▶

A timestamp is a specific point in time (like 2:14 PM), while duration is the "space" between two points (like 2 hours and 14 minutes). You can use a unix timestamp converter to turn those weird long numbers into readable dates.

Does this tool handle lunch breaks? ▶

The best way to handle breaks is to calculate your total duration and then use the "Subtract" feature to remove your 30 or 60-minute break. It keeps the math clean and easy to verify.

Is there a limit to how much time I can add? ▶

Not really! Whether you're adding 5 minutes for a study timer online session or 5,000 hours for a long-term engineering project, the time duration calculator handles the large integers without breaking a sweat.
