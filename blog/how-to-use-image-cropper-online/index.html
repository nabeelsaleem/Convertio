The "Rule of Thirds" Secret

Most people crop right to the center. Don't do that. Use the grid lines in the image cropper to place your subject on one of the intersecting lines. It makes the photo feel more "alive" and professional. It’s a trick I learned the hard way after ruining a high-stakes hero banner back in 2023.

Watch the Resolution!

When you crop too aggressively, you lose pixels. If you take a tiny corner of a photo and blow it up, it’ll look like a Minecraft block. Always start with the highest resolution possible.

Common mistakes I’ve made (so you don't)

I once sent a batch of 50 team photos to a developer without checking the metadata. They were perfectly cropped, but they still had all the GPS and camera info attached, which was a huge privacy no-no. Now, I always run my files through a tool to remove image metadata after I'm done cropping. It’s just good practice.

Another big one? Not checking the file size. If you’re a freelancer, you know that clients hate heavy images. I actually use a freelance hourly rate calculator to figure out how much time I’m wasting on these manual tasks. If a task takes more than 30 seconds, I find a tool to automate it.

But back to the image cropper. The biggest mistake is "Over-cropping." You want to leave some "breathing room" around your subject. If their head is touching the very top of the frame, it feels cramped. Give it some air!
