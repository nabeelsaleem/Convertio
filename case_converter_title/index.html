The Rules of Professional Title Capitalization

Title case isn't just about capitalizing every word. Standard rules dictate that short prepositions, articles, and conjunctions should remain lowercase unless they are the first or last word of the title. For example, "The Lord of the Rings" is correct, while "The Lord Of The Rings" looks amateurish. Our tool understands these nuances. Frequently Asked Questions

Title Case is a capitalization style where the first letter of every major word is capitalized. Minor words like 'a', 'the', 'and', and 'of' are usually kept in lowercase unless they are the first or last word of the title.

Commonly, short articles (a, an, the), coordinating conjunctions (and, but, for, nor, or, so, yet), and short prepositions (at, by, in, of, on, to, up) are not capitalized unless they start or end the title.
