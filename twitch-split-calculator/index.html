Understanding the Twitch Partner vs Affiliate Split

For many content creators, the dream is to turn streaming into a full-time career. However, understanding the twitch partner vs affiliate split is essential for financial planning. Twitch operates on a subscription model where viewers pay a monthly fee to support their favorite streamers. The revenue from these subscriptions is shared between Twitch and the creator, but the percentage you keep depends heavily on your status and contract.

The Standard 50/50 Split for Affiliates

When you first reach Twitch Affiliate status, you are placed on a standard 50/50 revenue split. This means if a viewer buys a Tier 1 subscription for $4.99, Twitch takes approximately $2.50, and you receive $2.50. It is important to note that Twitch deducts payment processing fees and local taxes before the split is calculated, which is why our Twitch sub revenue calculator includes an advanced setting for estimated fees.

How the 70/30 Partner Plus Split Works

In 2023, Twitch introduced the "Partner Plus" program (now expanded), allowing eligible creators to access a 70/30 Twitch split. This is a significant boost in income. To qualify, streamers must maintain a specific number of "Sub Points" from recurring paid subscriptions.

  • Tier 1 Sub: 1 Point
  • Tier 2 Sub: 2 Points
  • Tier 3 Sub: 6 Points

By using our calculator, you can see exactly how many Tier 3 subs you need to reach the 350-point threshold faster. Reaching this level can increase your take-home pay by 40% compared to the standard Affiliate split.

Hidden Factors: Regional Pricing and Taxes

One common point of confusion is "Local Sub Pricing." Twitch adjusted sub costs globally to make them more affordable in different regions. While a sub in the US is $4.99, it might be significantly less in other countries. This means your streamer revenue isn't just a flat calculation of sub count; it depends on where your audience lives.

Maximizing Your Twitch Earnings

To maximize your revenue, focus on converting "Prime" subs into recurring paid subs, as Prime subs do not count toward the Partner Plus point threshold. Additionally, encouraging Tier 2 and Tier 3 subs can help you reach revenue goals with a smaller, more dedicated audience.
