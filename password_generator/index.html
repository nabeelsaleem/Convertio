Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between static and dynamic QR codes? ‚ñ∂

Static QR codes have a permanent destination that cannot be changed. They're encoded directly and offer complete privacy. Dynamic QR codes use a short URL that redirects to your content, allowing you to change the destination anytime and track analytics. Dynamic codes cost more but offer flexibility for marketing campaigns.

How small can I print a QR code? ‚ñ∂

The minimum size is 2 x 2 cm (0.8 x 0.8 inches) for close scanning. For posters, divide the scanning distance by 10 to get the minimum size in centimeters. Always test before mass printing!

Can QR codes be customized with my brand colors? ‚ñ∂

Yes! Modern QR code generators allow color customization while maintaining scannability. The key is ensuring sufficient contrast between the code and background. Our tool includes automatic contrast checking to ensure reliability.

Are QR codes secure? Can they be hacked? ‚ñ∂

The QR code itself is just data and cannot be "hacked." The security risk lies in where it points. Always verify your QR code links to your intended destination. For sensitive applications, use static codes that you control completely, or password-protected dynamic codes.

What file format should I use for print vs. digital? ‚ñ∂

For print: Always use vector formats like SVG or EPS for infinite scalability. For digital: PNG at 2x or 3x resolution works well. Never use a low-resolution PNG for print‚Äîit will become pixelated and unscannable.

Can I track how many people scan my QR code? ‚ñ∂

Only with dynamic QR codes. They provide analytics on scan location, device type, time, and unique vs. repeat scans. Static codes don't offer built-in analytics, but you can use a URL shortener like Bitly to track clicks on the destination URL.
