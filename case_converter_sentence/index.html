How the Sentence Case Logic Works

Sentence case is the standard format for most English writing. It involves capitalizing the first letter of the first word of a sentence and keeping the rest in lowercase (except for proper nouns). Our algorithm intelligently detects sentence endings—such as periods, exclamation marks, and question marks—and ensures the following word begins with a capital letter. For even more detailed text analysis, you might want to use our Sentence Counter to track your document's structure.
