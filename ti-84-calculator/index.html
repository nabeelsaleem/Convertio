What Is the TI-84 Calculator?

The TI-84 calculator is a graphing calculator manufactured by Texas Instruments and is one of the most widely used educational calculators in the world. First introduced in 2004 as a successor to the TI-83 Plus, the TI-84 Plus has become the gold standard for high school and college mathematics. Whether you're working through algebra, trigonometry, statistics, or calculus, the ti 84 calculator texas instruments handles it all.

Key TI-84 Model Lineup

Texas Instruments has released several versions of the TI-84. The most common models are the TI-84 Plus (standard), the TI-84 Plus Silver Edition (higher memory), and the flagship TI-84 Plus CE (full color screen, rechargeable battery via mini-USB). All models are eligible for use on standardized tests like the SAT and ACT.

TI-84 Plus CE vs. TI-84 Plus: What's the Difference?

The most significant difference between the TI-84 Plus CE and the older TI-84 Plus is the display. The CE model features a full-color backlit LCD screen, making graphs and plots significantly easier to read. The CE is also thinner, lighter, and uses a rechargeable battery charged via a standard mini-USB cable — the same type used for the ti 84 graphing calculator charger. The CE also supports the Python programming language through an optional app.

The older TI-84 Plus Silver Edition offered more RAM and archive memory than the base model, and came in a range of ti 84 calculator colors including ti 84 calculator pink and ti 84 calculator purple. These became popular collectibles and remain widely used today.

Is the TI-84 Plus CE Allowed on the SAT and ACT?

Yes. The TI-84 Plus CE is an approved calculator for both the SAT and the ACT. It does not have a CAS (Computer Algebra System), which is the key reason it qualifies. The only exception is the TI-84 Plus CE Python edition — for this model, the Python app must be removed before the exam. Our ti 84 calculator online replicates the same allowed feature set.
