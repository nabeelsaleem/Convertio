How PayPal Fees Are Calculated

The standard formula for a PayPal transaction fee is:

Total Fee = (Transaction Amount × Fee Percentage) + Fixed Fee

For example, in the United States, a standard domestic transaction often carries a 2.99% fee plus a $0.49 fixed fee. If you sell an item for $100, PayPal takes $2.99 (the percentage) plus $0.49 (the fixed fee), totaling $3.48. Your net payout would be $96.52. Domestic vs. International PayPal Fees

International payments are more expensive due to cross-border fees and currency conversion costs. While a domestic US payment might be around 2.99%, an international payment can jump to 4.49% or higher. Tips to Manage and Reduce Transaction Fees

  • Request "Friends & Family": Only for personal transfers between trusted individuals, as this avoids fees but also removes purchase protection.
  • Adjust Your Pricing: Use the "To Receive" feature in our calculator to build the fee into your service price.
  • Use QR Codes: PayPal often offers lower rates for in-person QR code payments compared to online invoices.
  • Withdraw Less Frequently: While withdrawal to a bank is usually free, some instant transfer options carry additional costs.
