How to Calculate Overtime Pay

The formula for overtime is straightforward but easy to mess up manually:

  1. Regular Pay: Multiply your hourly rate by your regular hours (usually 40).
  2. Overtime Rate: Multiply your hourly rate by the multiplier (e.g., $20 x 1.5 = $30).
  3. Overtime Pay: Multiply the overtime rate by the number of overtime hours worked.
  4. Total Pay: Add your regular pay and overtime pay together.

Overtime Pay – Frequently Asked Questions

In the United States, under the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA), overtime is generally any work performed beyond 40 hours in a single workweek. Some states, like California, also have daily overtime rules for work exceeding 8 hours in a day.

Simply multiply your base hourly wage by 1.5. For example, if you earn $20 per hour, your overtime rate is $20 × 1.5 = $30 per hour. Our calculator does this automatically for you.

Overtime pay is considered ordinary income by the IRS. However, because it increases your total earnings, it might be withheld at a higher rate on your paycheck if it pushes you into a higher tax bracket for that specific pay period.
