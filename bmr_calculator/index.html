Understanding Your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)

Your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) represents the minimum amount of energy (measured in calories) your body requires to function while at rest. Even when you are sleeping or sitting perfectly still, your body is busy pumping blood, breathing, and repairing cells. Our BMR Calculator uses the scientifically validated Mifflin-St Jeor equation to give you a precise estimate of these baseline needs.

Why Calculate Your BMR?

Knowing your BMR is the first step toward any fitness or weight management goal. If you want to lose weight, you need to consume fewer calories than your Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE). If you want to gain muscle, you need a surplus. By combining this tool with our Age Calculator to ensure your inputs are precise, you can create a highly customized nutrition plan.

BMR vs. BMI: What's the Difference?

While BMR tells you how much energy you burn, the BMI Calculator tells you if your weight is in a healthy range for your height. Both are useful metrics, but BMR is more specific to your metabolic health and daily caloric requirements.
