Why Calculate Percentage Growth?

Raw numbers often don't tell the whole story. For example, an increase of $10 is significant if you started with $20 (a 50% increase), but negligible if you started with $1,000 (a 1% increase). By using a percent growth calculator, you gain a standardized perspective on how much a value has truly changed.

The Percentage Increase Formula

If you prefer to do the math manually, the formula for percentage increase is:

Percentage Increase = [(Final Value - Initial Value) / |Initial Value|] × 100

First, subtract the original number from the new number. Then, divide that difference by the absolute value of the original number. Finally, multiply by 100 to get the percentage. Our tool automates this entire process, eliminating the risk of human error.
