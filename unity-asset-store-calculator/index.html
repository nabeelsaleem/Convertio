How to Use the Unity Developer Payout Calculator

Selling digital assets on the Unity Asset Store is one of the most sustainable ways for game developers to generate passive income. However, understanding exactly how much will land in your bank account requires navigating platform fees and potential taxes. Our unity developer payout calculator simplifies this process by providing an instant breakdown of your gross revenue versus net earnings.

Understanding the Unity Asset Store Fee Structure

Unity operates on a standard revenue-share model. For most creators, the platform takes a 30% commission on every sale. This fee covers the hosting, payment processing, and global distribution of your assets. While 70% goes to the developer, there are other factors to consider, such as VAT (Value Added Tax) for European customers or withholding taxes depending on your country of residence.

Using an asset store fee calculator helps you price your products competitively. If you want to earn a specific amount per sale, you must factor in the 30% cut. For example, to net $35 per sale, you would need to list your asset at $50.

Maximizing Your Game Dev Income

To increase your payout, focus on high-demand categories like 3D models, specialized shaders, or complete project templates. Many developers use our tool alongside the Freelance Hourly Rate Calculator to determine if the time spent developing an asset is worth the projected long-term royalties.

  • Bundle Assets: Creating packs often leads to higher average order values.
  • Seasonal Sales: Participate in Unity's official sales to boost volume, even if the per-unit profit is lower.
  • Update Regularly: Assets that support the latest Unity LTS versions rank higher in search results.

Taxes and Payout Thresholds

It is important to remember that Unity has a minimum payout threshold. If you choose wire transfer, you typically need to accumulate $100 before a payment is triggered. Furthermore, if you are an international developer selling to US customers, you may be subject to US withholding tax unless your country has a tax treaty. Our advanced settings allow you to simulate these deductions so you aren't surprised when your monthly statement arrives.

Unity Payout – Frequently Asked Questions

Unity takes a 30% cut of the gross sales price. The developer receives the remaining 70%.

Payouts are processed monthly, usually around the 15th of each month for the previous month's earnings, provided the minimum threshold is met.

Beyond the 30% commission, you may encounter bank wire transfer fees (often $15-$25) or currency conversion fees depending on your bank and payout method.
