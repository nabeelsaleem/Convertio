Understanding CGST, SGST, and IGST

One of the most common points of confusion in the Indian GST system is the different types of taxes. Our calculator breaks these down for you:

  • CGST (Central Goods and Services Tax): Collected by the Central Government on intra-state sales.
  • SGST (State Goods and Services Tax): Collected by the State Government on intra-state sales.
  • IGST (Integrated Goods and Services Tax): Collected by the Central Government for inter-state transactions.

When you sell within your own state, the GST is split equally between CGST and SGST. For example, on an 18% slab, 9% goes to the Center and 9% to the State. Our tool calculates these splits automatically, saving you from manual errors.

Why the 18% GST Slab is Most Common

The 18% rate is often referred to as the "standard" rate because it covers the majority of services and manufactured goods in India. From IT services and telecommunications to branded garments and electronic items, the 18% slab is the most frequently used. GST Calculation Formula

For those who prefer to know the math behind the tool:
To Add GST: GST Amount = (Original Cost * GST%) / 100.
To Remove GST: GST Amount = Total Cost - (Total Cost / (1 + GST%/100)).
